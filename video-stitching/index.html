🎬 Video Stitching

Given a set of video clips, find the minimum number needed to cover the entire event. Watch the greedy algorithm select optimal clips in real-time!

Problem Statement
You are given a series of video clips from a sporting event that lasted time seconds. Each clip clips[i] = [start_i, end_i] covers the interval [start_i, end_i]. Clips can overlap and be cut freely.

Return the minimum number of clips needed to cover the entire event [0, time]. If impossible, return -1.

Algorithm Strategy
1. Sort clips by start time (ascending), breaking ties by end time (descending — longest reach first).

2. Greedy Scan — maintain a cursor at the current coverage end. For all clips that start ≤ cursor, find the one that extends farthest.

3. Choose that clip, advance the cursor, increment the count. Repeat.

4. Gap? If no clip can reach from the cursor, it's impossible → return -1.

💡 Gospel Connection — Covering the Whole with Many Parts

In Video Stitching, no single clip may cover the entire event — but by carefully selecting and combining overlapping segments, we can achieve complete coverage. Each clip is valuable not for what it covers alone, but for how it connects to others.

"For the body is not one member, but many... And the eye cannot say unto the hand, I have no need of thee: nor again the head to the feet, I have no need of you."
— 1 Corinthians 12:14, 21

The greedy insight teaches that wise selection matters. At each step we don't just pick any clip — we pick the one that reaches farthest. Similarly, we are counseled to make the most of every opportunity: "See then that ye walk circumspectly, not as fools, but as wise, redeeming the time" (Ephesians 5:15–16). When each member contributes their best reach, the whole body of Christ is covered.
